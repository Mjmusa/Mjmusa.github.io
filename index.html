Research/School Projects

MRI-Compatible Parallel Robot

The primary motivation for developing this robot is to create a general-purpose robotic platform capable of producing accurate 6 DoF motion inside the MRI bore to preform needle-based interventional procedures (i.e., radio-frequency ablation, biopsy) or generate accurate motion for other MRI-based experiments (i.e., motion compensation imaging sequence development, HIFU probe manipulation). The robot is driven by 6 pneumatic cylinder actuators and controlled via a robust sliding mode controller. The low-level controller is capable of positioning the pneumatic actuators with sub-millimeter accuracy and a tissue mimicry targeting experiment demonstrated the feasibility of the robot for needle insertion procedures.

accepted for publishing in International Symposium on Medical Robotics (ISMR 2021)

Liver Cancer Ablation Robot

In this work, a 5 DoF patient-mounted robot was designed and fabricated for the treatment of primary liver cancer. The robot consists of two dual cartesian stages used to control the 4 DoF pose of a needle insertion module. The needle insertion module is a 3D printed flexible fluidic actuator capable of a step-like, grasp-insert-release actuation that mimics the manual insertion procedure currently used in clinical practice.

see journal publication in IEEE TMECH

Tool-Tissue Interactive Force Modelling

For my final project in the course BMEG 5213 Tissue Mechanics, viscoelastic tissue modeling was used to develop a tool-tissue interaction model capable of predicting the forces acting on a needle is inserted into tissue. The model was compared to both a phantom experiment and ex-vivo porcine liver experiment.

NASA Robotics Mining Competition

For my senior design project, I served as the lead mechanical engineer for the excavation subsystem of the University of Arkansas NASA Robotics Mining Competition Team, Razorbotz. The goal of the project is to design and build a robot capable of excavating resources in a simulated Martian environment.
